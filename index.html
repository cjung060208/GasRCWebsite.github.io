My RC Gas powered boat

A favorite project of mine. It is 1 meter long, has a hull made of fiberglass, and is powered by a 26cc 2-stroke engine.

  • More

Making the Hull

I first 3D printed molds of my CAD scaled to 6 inches long to test how many layers of fiberglass I needed to make a strong hull. I settled on 6 layers of 6 oz fiberglass.

I then made the real thing using a mold. It took a long time and was pretty difficult because it was the first time I used composite materials. The end product was not the best as it had air bubbles but it was solid and worked.

Popping the hull and touching it up

I sanded the inside and outside removing any bumps and ensuring smoothness. The gas mask might have been overkill but I didn't want to inhale any fiberglass particles and I just had one laying around. I then filled in any voids with epoxy and cut off the excess fiberglass to make the final product.

The Engine

My engine is a Rc Marine 2-Stroke 26cc Engine. It is a replica of the Zenoah G260PUM but a lot more affordable. Even though it is a Chinese replica I have had minimal issues with it with most being my fault and the rest being on the carburetor. It makes a lot of power and is fun to work with.

Combining Everything

I bought a few more things (stinger, flexshaft, rudder, servos) and then combined everything I had. I made some dumb decisions like drilling holes in the transom bigger than needed for the wires and designing the way this servo controls the throttle in this really weird way but overall it worked. The tubing is also a mess because of all the weird connections and different sizes there are but I decided not to touch it if it worked.

Semi Finished Product

I made a temporary roof for it with foam board for its first test run. It ran well and fast but unfortunately I couldn't get a video of it because I wasn't skilled enough to record and steer at the same time. I later took the foam board off and am currently making a permament 3D printed roof for it (CAD is done and it just needs to be printed).
